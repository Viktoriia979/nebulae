Nebulae:

Nebulae are huge clouds of gas and dust found in space. They can be many light-years wide and come in different colours and shapes. Some nebulae glow because they are lit up by nearby stars, while others are dark because they block the light behind them. They are often called the "nurseries" of stars because this is where many new stars begin.

Nebulae form in different ways. Some are created when massive stars explode in a supernova, sending gas and dust out into space. Over millions of years, gravity pulls this material together into large clouds. As the gas becomes denser, it heats up and eventually forms new stars. Some of the leftover material can also become planets.

Nebulae are important because they play a key role in the life cycle of stars. They provide the materials needed to create new stars and planetary systems. By studying nebulae, astronomers can learn how stars form, evolve, and die. This helps scientists better understand the universe and how our own Solar System may have formed billions of years ago.
